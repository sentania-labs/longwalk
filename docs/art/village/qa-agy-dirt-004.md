## Ranked Remaining Tells

1. **Flat Dirt Core (Dominant)** - Visible at all zooms (0.5x, 1x, 2x)
   While the shoulders now have high-frequency detail, the large pooled clearing in front of the buildings (the protected core) remains visually dead flat. The bounded broad tonal drift is not sufficient to replicate the worn, pebbly earth of the spike. Because this clearing is large and central, its flatness dominates the scene, and the stark contrast between the textured shoulders and the flat core pulls the result strongly away from being confusable.

2. **Retinted Grass Morphology** - Visible at 1x, 2x
   The high-frequency detail applied to the shoulders reads heavily as retinted grass rather than worn earth. Because it uses the grass luminance as a substrate, the dirt takes on the tufted, leafy morphology of the grass instead of looking like gravel or packed earth.

3. **Aliasing / Shimmer / Synthetic Noise** - Visible at 0.5x
   The high-frequency detail on the shoulders aliases poorly when downsampled, creating a synthetic noise and digital-static tell along the lane edges at the 0.5x zoom level.
