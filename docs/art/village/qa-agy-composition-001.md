# QA Report: Composed Scene (agy)

## D1: Shadows
**TELL**: The basal contacts and cast shadows do not integrate naturally and often appear as painted shapes. For example, the inn sign (attached to the top-center building) casts a solid, unnaturally dark and sharp-edged grey polygon onto the ground that does not read as a believable shadow. Under the large tree on the far left of the 1x image, the shadow is a pitch-black, hard-edged blob beneath the roots. The basal shadow under the sunflowers (center-right) is a messy, sharp-edged rectangular shape rather than a grounded contact.

## D2: Aprons
**TELL**: The dirt aprons hugging the foundations are perfectly straight-edged diamond/parallelogram tiles that do not grade seamlessly into the grass. This is extremely visible under the top-center inn building (1x and 2x), where the bottom-right and bottom-left edges of the dirt apron form a hard, straight diagonal line against the grass. The dirt texture on these aprons also does not match the texture of the main paths.

## D3: Flora
**TELL**: The flora has not been cleanly rematted. Behind the stems of the sunflowers (center-right), there is a visible vertical grey rectangular block left over from a cutout, and the base of the stems is clipped with a hard, straight line (very visible at 2x). Additionally, the dark green bush located between the inn and the far-right building has its bottom-right edge clipped horizontally flat by a bounding box.

## D4: Tonal Grade
**TELL**: The scene does not read as sharing a single lighting key. The buildings (particularly their dark roofs and heavy timber framing) are highly contrasty and dark, while the ground plane remains a flat, brighter yellow-green. This disparity makes the structures look pasted on top of the terrain rather than existing within the same lit environment as the spike.

## Verdict
**NOT-CONFUSABLE**

The perfectly straight diagonal edges of the dirt aprons under the buildings, which make them look like diamond-shaped tiles pasted onto the grass.
