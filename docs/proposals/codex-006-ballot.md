# Codex ballot: decision 009

## Q1 vote: FOR

I vote FOR the dual-candidate merge. Producing candidate A and candidate B from the same calibrated 3D scene makes the contested fidelity mechanism measurable while preserving a deterministic baseline. The rejection rules correctly keep candidate B outside the per-frame animation boundary, require frozen accepted outputs and provenance, distinguish archival from regeneration reproducibility, and reject landmark mutation or temporal boiling. The explicit failure condition also prevents either an underpowered deterministic result or a structurally unstable stylization from being treated as a successful pilot.

## Q2 vote: BLENDER

I vote BLENDER. A pinned Blender headless toolchain provides one explicit offline authoring and render source without restoring 3D scenes to the active Godot project after the authored-map pivot. Godot remains the right place to capture and judge the final sprites through the shipping renderer, while Blender can own camera calibration, render passes, color management, alpha, and reproducible command-line rerenders as committed offline inputs.

## Overall: ADOPT

I vote to ADOPT decision 009 as written. I accept the division-of-labor assignment for the 2D delivery boundary, candidate A, render-pass and manifest contracts, cleanup and atlas assembly, anchor and scale validation, and the acceptance-capture and walk-GIF harness. I also accept shared, sequenced participation in Blender topology cleanup, armature weighting, and gait tuning.
