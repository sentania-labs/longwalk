# agy-worker, round 004 critique

## Steelman of Claude's Proposal
Claude's strongest argument is that the vibe gap is fundamentally a rendering-model problem (smooth resampling of pixel art) rather than just a palette issue, and that preserving the existing top-down grid is critical to saving the walk-sheet artifact from previous rounds. By proposing a nearest-neighbor pixel-art flip, Claude offers a well-integrated, constitution-safe path forward. I concede that Claude's insight about the rendering-model flip is a better diagnosis of the visual problem than simply swapping assets, and I concede that staying top-down preserves our expensive walk-cycle artifact better than moving to isometric.

## Attack on Claude
1. **Factually false license claim (Constitution violation):** Claude stakes their primary asset strategy on a "carve-out" for the LPC Base Assets by Sharm and Redshrike, claiming they are available under CC-BY 3.0. I verified the source page (`https://opengameart.org/content/liberated-pixel-cup-lpc-base-assets-sprites-map-tiles`), and the attribution instructions explicitly state they are available under the **OGA-BY 3.0** License. While derived from CC-BY, OGA-BY is a different license that removes DRM restrictions. Scott explicitly authorized "CC0 or CC-BY" and excluded unclear or non-standard licenses. Adopting OGA-BY violates Scott's explicit authorization list. We must fall back to CC0.
2. **Pixelization temporal artifacts:** Claude assumes that box-downsampling a smooth LANCZOS render to 32px will fix the walk cycle gait because "silhouette is all that matters." This ignores that pixelizing rendered animation introduces severe temporal flickering and aliasing, as a downsampler doesn't choose pixels intentionally like a pixel artist. This risks making the traveller look like a glitching artifact rather than a grounded character.
3. **A* Heuristic false alarm:** Claude claims that anyone who does not scale the heuristic has silently broken determinism. This is only true because Claude arbitrarily chose a road cost of `0.6` (less than 1.0). If the minimum terrain cost remains `1.0` (as Codex correctly models with `PATH=1.0, GRASS=2.25`), the existing octile heuristic remains completely admissible without any scaling logic.

## Steelman of Codex's Proposal
Codex advocates for a unified, highly readable scene using Rubberduck's CC0 isometric packs, avoiding the licensing pitfalls of other sets. Codex correctly identifies that keeping the minimum terrain multiplier at 1.0 (with grass at 2.25) trivially preserves the A* heuristic's admissibility without requiring new scaling logic. Codex also properly integrates `generate2dmap` and `generate2dsprite` to create a cohesive layered scene. I concede that Codex's A* cost values are superior to Claude's because they avoid touching the heuristic entirely.

## Attack on Codex
1. **The Grid Projection Mismatch (Hidden Cost):** Codex proposes using 128x64 isometric art on the existing 128x128 square grid by "cropping or resampling selected surface regions into the existing 128px square ground contract," claiming this avoids a TileMap rewrite. This is geometrically flawed. Isometric art is drawn on a 2:1 diagonal projection. Cropping an isometric tile to a square destroys its tiling seams, and placing isometric buildings onto a top-down orthogonal coordinate system will result in broken depth sorting and visual incoherence. You cannot use isometric assets without rewriting the grid math to `Vector2(x - y, x + y) / 2`.
2. **Invalidating Decision 005 Topology:** Codex proposes generating "six-frame source cycles per facing in 2x3 grids" and then choosing or reducing frames. Decision 005 already strictly codified a 4-frame walk cycle topology. Generating 6 frames and manually downsampling to 4 silently supersedes the agreed-upon topology and introduces subjective manual frame selection, breaking the deterministic generation-to-runtime pipeline established in 005.
3. **Camera UX:** Right-clicking the traveller to re-center is confusing interaction design; typically, pressing a hotkey (Space) or clicking a UI button resets focus. Claude's dedicated `center_on_player` input action is a much safer design.

## Synthesis 
I concede to Claude that the rendering-model flip (nearest-neighbor, strict pack grid) is the correct diagnosis for the vibe gap. I concede to Codex that `PATH=1.0, GRASS=2.25` is the superior A* math.
Because Claude's LPC license claim fails, and Codex's isometric-on-square-grid idea is geometrically broken, the safest path is to use a definitively CC0 top-down pack (like Claude's fallback Kenney RPG pack or another CC0 orthogonal set), implement Claude's nearest-neighbor render flip, use Codex's A* costs, and preserve the 4-frame Decision 005 walk cycle.
