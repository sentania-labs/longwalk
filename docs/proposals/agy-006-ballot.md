# Round 006, Phase 3: Antigravity Ballot

## Q1 Vote: FOR
I vote FOR the dual-candidate merge. Testing two distinct, heavily-constrained candidates (deterministic baseline versus static-texture/single-pass generative) under strict rejection rules is the only empirical way to verify if the painterly spike can be recovered without introducing the temporal boiling we all agreed is a disqualifier. Deferring the final choice to measured evidence rather than a premature commitment de-risks the pipeline.

## Q2 Vote: BLENDER
I vote for BLENDER. While a Godot sub-viewport would guarantee exact shader matching, the orchestrator's point regarding the 2026-07-15 pivot is overriding. Reintroducing 3D scenes into the Godot project, even strictly for rendering, risks violating the architectural separation of the new authored 2D model. Keeping all 3D assets, rigs, and rendering entirely offline in Blender guarantees a clean boundary and ensures no 3D logic bleeds into the shipped game.
